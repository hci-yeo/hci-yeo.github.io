About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Digit Robotics, exploring the future of Natural Human-Robot Interaction.

Previously, I worked as a Research Scientist at Huawei through the TopMinds program and conducted research at KAIST.

I received my PhD from the University of St Andrews under the supervision of Prof. Aaron Quigley. I also completed internships at Microsoft Research Cambridge, Google, and Tokyo Tech.

My research focuses on developing novel input devices and interaction techniques for next-generation computing systems. I work at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, Ubiquitous Computing, and Wearable Technologies, utilizing machine learning and signal processing to unlock new capabilities from low-cost sensors. My work has been published in premier conferences including ACM CHI, UIST, UbiComp, MobileHCI, IEEE VR, ISMAR, and SIGGRAPH.
